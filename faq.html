Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about ordering, pickup, and what we make.

Ordering & Pickup

Everything we make is baked to order — we don't keep a backstock or make extras "just in case." The Monday cutoff gives us exactly the right amount of time to source fresh ingredients, prep, and bake each item with care.

This also means we produce zero waste. No unsold pastries, no day-old sweets — just fresh food made specifically for the people who ordered it. We think that's the way it should be.

Orders open 10 days before each Saturday pickup. The banner at the top of every page always shows the current status — you can also check the Order page directly.

We recommend following us on Instagram to get notified when orders open each cycle.

Pickup is every other Saturday, 11am–1pm, from our home in Santa Clara County. Once your order is placed and paid, we'll email you the exact pickup address.

Please arrive during the pickup window — we can't hold orders for late pickups. If you have a conflict, reach out in advance and we'll do our best to accommodate.

Because everything is made to order and ingredients are purchased based on confirmed orders, we can't accept cancellations or changes after the order cutoff.

If something comes up before the cutoff, email us at hello@yapabakery.com and we'll work with you.

Payment

We accept all major credit and debit cards through Stripe — Visa, Mastercard, American Express, and Discover. Apple Pay and Google Pay are also accepted on supported devices.

Payment is required at order time. We don't hold orders without payment.

We're a small home kitchen operation — we buy ingredients specifically for confirmed orders. Pre-payment protects us from the real cost of a no-show and keeps our prices as fair as possible.

It also means your order is 100% confirmed once payment goes through. No guessing, no "I think I'm coming" uncertainty.

Food & Dietary Needs

Yes! Our Vegan Butter Cookies are fully plant-based. We're happy to discuss other dietary needs — just add a note in your order or email us in advance.

Please be aware that our kitchen handles wheat, dairy, eggs, nuts, and other common allergens. We take care, but we can't guarantee a fully allergen-free environment.

Our salteñas are made with a traditional Bolivian recipe: a lightly sweet dough encasing a slow-braised filling of seasoned beef or chicken, diced potato, green onion, olives, and a touch of heat from Bolivian peppers.

They're baked, never fried. If you have a specific allergy or need a filling substitution, let us know in the order notes and we'll do our best.

Bulk & Event Orders

Absolutely — we love being part of celebrations. For bulk orders (25+ salteñas, full cakes, large cookie trays) or event catering inquiries, please use our event inquiry form at least 2–3 weeks in advance.

Bulk pricing and custom options are available. We'll reply within 2 business days to discuss details.

Currently we offer pickup only from our home in Santa Clara County. Delivery may be available for large event orders — ask in your inquiry and we'll let you know.

Home Kitchen Operations

MEHKO stands for Microenterprise Home Kitchen Operation — a California law (AB 626) that allows small food businesses to operate legally out of a licensed residential kitchen.

Yes, our kitchen is permitted and inspected through Santa Clara County. Our permit number is displayed in the footer of every page. California state law requires us to disclose: This food is made in a home kitchen and is not inspected by the Department of State Health Services or the local environmental health department.
