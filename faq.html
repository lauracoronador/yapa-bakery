A salteña is Bolivia's national street food — a hand-crimped pastry with a slow-braised filling of seasoned beef or chicken, potato, olives, and a touch of Bolivian heat. The dough is slightly sweet and golden. It's baked, never fried. And there's broth inside — which is the best part.
