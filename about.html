Made from scratch,
shared with heart.

Yapa Bakery is a home kitchen food business rooted in the flavors of Bolivia and the warmth of making something by hand for people you care about.

It started simply: I grew up eating Salteñas and knowing that the best food is made slowly, with attention and love. When I came to California, I wanted to bring those flavors with me — and share them with my community here.

"Every time I make a batch of Salteñas, I'm back in Bolivia. The smell of the dough, the way the filling comes together — it's memory and home in every bite."

Today, Yapa Bakery operates out of my licensed home kitchen in Santa Clara County, offering pre-order pickup twice a month. Everything is made in small batches — never in bulk — so each order gets the attention it deserves.

yapa

A little something extra.

In the Quechua language spoken across the Andes, yapa (or llapa) refers to the small gift a vendor adds to your purchase — a little extra thrown in with a smile. It's an act of generosity, of hospitality, of saying: I see you, and I want to give you a little more.

That's the spirit behind everything we bake. Not just a transaction — a moment of connection. Whether it's an extra alfajor tucked into your box or the care that goes into each crimp of a Salteña, yapa is in everything we do.

Rooted in Bolivia.

Bolivia is a landlocked country in the heart of South America — home to the world's largest salt flat, ancient Inca ruins, and one of the most vibrant food cultures on the continent.

The Salteña is Bolivia's national street food. You eat them in the morning, standing at a corner stall, carefully tilting the pastry so the broth doesn't spill. Each region has its own version — some spicy, some sweet, some with olives and hardboiled eggs. Every family has their recipe.

Our recipe carries that tradition — slow-braised filling, a lightly sweet dough, hand-crimped edges — made with the same care it takes to make them at home, because that's exactly where they're made.
